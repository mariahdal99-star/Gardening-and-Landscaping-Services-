Essential Garden Care

1. On Watering

Never stop showing a plant it is cared for. A little bit of water every day is better than a flood once a month. Consistency is how life learns to trust the sun again.

2. Soil Quality

The foundation is everything. You cannot grow something beautiful in exhausted soil. You must feed the ground with memories, shared laughter, and forgiveness before the first seed even touches the dirt.

3. Pruning

Sometimes you have to cut away the parts that are dead, even if it hurts. It’s not a punishment; it’s making room for a new version of us to reach for the sky.

My Personal Journal

I planted a perennial today. They say these are the hardest because they require you to stay in one place for a long time. I think I'm finally ready to stop wandering. I want to see how tall we can grow if we never leave each other's side.

The lilies are open. They remind me of the way you look when you're surprised. I've realized that the roses remember every kind word I've ever whispered to them. They bloom bigger for those who love them loudly.

The Seasons of Us

Winter: Do not be afraid of the quiet. Some things need to sleep before they can belong to the spring. Just because you don't see the growth doesn't mean the roots aren't deepening in the dark.

Spring: The vulnerability of a new leaf is the bravest thing in the world. Open your heart. The frost is over.

Autumn: There is beauty in letting go. Watch how gracefully the trees shed what they no longer need. They do not mourn the leaves; they thank them for the shade they gave.
