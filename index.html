🌼 Comprehensive Lily Growing Guide

1. Choosing the Right Lily

Lilies (Lilium spp.) come in many varieties. Beginners should start with Asiatic lilies as they are hardy and reliable.

2. Sunlight Requirements

6-8 hours of direct sun is best. Flowers love sun; roots love shade.

3. Soil Preparation

Soil pH should be 6.0 to 6.8. Well-draining soil is vital to prevent bulb rot.

4. Planting:

15-20cm deep. Pointy end up.

5. Watering:

Deeply at the base once or twice a week.

6. Fertilizing:

Use 10-10-10 when shoots first emerge.

7. Pests:

Monitor for red lily beetles and aphids daily.

8. Deadheading:

Remove spent flowers but keep green stems.

9. Dormancy:

Only cut stems once they turn completely yellow.

10. Division:

Lift and divide bulbs every 3-4 years in Autumn.

1. Propagation by Bulb Division

Best for mature lilies that have formed daughter bulbs. This is the most reliable method for beginners.

Steps: 1 Dig around the bulb. 2 Lift gently. 3 Separate daughter bulbs by twisting. 4 Replant immediately.

Planting Depth Guide
Bulb Height: 3cmPlant at: 6–9 cm
Bulb Height: 5cmPlant at: 10–15 cm

2. Propagation by Scaling

A single bulb can produce dozens of offspring. Pull outer scales downward from the basal plate.

Process: Place scales in a bag with moist vermiculite. Store at 20–25°C for 6–8 weeks until bulblets form. Transfer to 2–5°C for 6 weeks (cold period) before potting.

3. Propagation by Bulbils

Produced in leaf axils along the stem (e.g., Tiger Lilies). Harvest when they detach easily. Plant 2–3 cm deep. They generally produce leaves in year 1 and flowers in year 2–3.

4. Propagation by Seed

Slow but allows for new hybrids. Collect pods when they split naturally. Epigeal: Leaves emerge shortly. Hypogeal: Bulb develops first below ground.
